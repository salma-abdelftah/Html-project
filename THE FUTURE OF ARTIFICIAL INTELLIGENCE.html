THE FUTURE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Building intelligent systems and ultimately, automating intelligence remains a daunting task, and one that may take decades to fully realize. AI research is currently focused on addressing existing shortcomings, such as the ability of AI systems to converse in natural language and to perceive and respond to their environment. However, the search for AI has grown into a field with far-reaching applications, many of which are considered indispensable and are already taken for granted. Nearly all industrial, governmental, and consumer applications are likely to utilize AI capabilities in the future. The possible future uses of AI devices in military combat situations have raised ethical concerns (see Warfare). Robot devices such as unmanned aerial vehicles and small robotic ground vehicles have been used in military situations for reconnaissance and to attack targets with deadly force while the device was under the control of a human operator, sometimes at a remote location. The decision to destroy a target is made by a human being. However, AI technology is under development that would allow military robots to act more autonomously, including analyzing terrain and recognizing targets. Such robots could potentially make decisions to attack targets and use deadly force, meaning they would need to distinguish friendly combatants and noncombatants from enemies. Such a capability is currently beyond any AI system. Some experts have proposed a ban on such autonomous weapons systems under international law.
